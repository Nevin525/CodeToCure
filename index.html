What is KCNH5?

What our children are up against.

KCNH5 epilepsy is rare and severe. A change in a single gene can reshape a child's earliest years with seizures and developmental delays. Understanding it is the first step toward changing it.

What it is
Rare genetic epilepsy
When it starts
Early childhood
What it brings
Recurrent seizures, developmental delay
Why it happens
A change in the KCNH5 gene

Research

Two paths to a cure.

Your support helps fund research that goes after the root cause of KCNH5 epilepsy, not just the symptoms, so children have a real chance at a healthier future.

Approach 01

Antisense oligonucleotide (ASO) therapy

A medicine designed to quiet the faulty genetic instructions behind KCNH5 seizures before they ever start, treating the cause instead of masking it.

Silence the signal
Approach 02

CRISPR gene correction

Research working toward gently rewriting the changed gene itself, restoring healthy function right where the disorder begins.

Rewrite the code
